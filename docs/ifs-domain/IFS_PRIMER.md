# Inner Atlas — IFS Domain Knowledge Primer

## The Self

NOT a part. The innate, undamaged core of every person.
- 8 Cs: Calm, Curious, Compassionate, Connected, Confident, Creative, Courageous, Clear
- 5 Ps: Presence, Patience, Perspective, Persistence, Playfulness
- Self-energy = degree of Self access vs. blending with a part
- Goal of IFS: Self-leadership — Self leading the system with the 8 Cs

## Part Types

### MANAGERS — Proactive Protectors (#3B5BA5, rounded rect)
Work preemptively to keep system functioning and prevent exile activation.
Often feel like "normal personality" — hardest to see as parts.
19 types in Inner Atlas: Perfectionist, Analyzer, Planner, Controller, Pleaser,
Caretaker, Peacemaker, Minimizer, Hypervigilant, Skeptic, Rule-Keeper, Rationalizer,
Restrainer, Comparer, Suppressor, Tension-Holder, Optimist, Timer, Inner Critic

### FIREFIGHTERS — Reactive Protectors (#C2600A, starburst)
Activated when exiles break through. Act impulsively to suppress exile pain.
Often feel "out of control" to the person. Frequently shamed by Managers.
16 types: Substance User, Behavior Addict, Rage-Expresser, Dissociator, Self-Harmer,
Impulsive Spender, Sexual Compulsive, Binge Eater, Risk-Taker, Withdrawer, Avoidant,
Daydreamer, Deflector, Rebel, Distractor, Physical-Agitator

### EXILES — The Wounded Parts (#7C3D9B, circle)
Young parts carrying burdens from past experiences. Kept away by protectors.
Healing exiles (unburdening) is the deepest IFS work.
17 types: Abandoned Child, Shamed One, Terrified One, Lonely One, Invisible One,
Helpless One, Unlovable One, Worthless One, Betrayed One, Powerless One,
Grief-Carrier, Rage-Carrier (young), Humiliated One, Burdened One,
Collapsed One, Frozen One, Trembling One

## Key Processes

- Blending: part merges with Self — person feels "taken over"
- Unblending: creating space between Self and part — accessing Self-energy
- Trailhead: any activation that leads inward
- Protection Chain: Manager/Firefighter -> Exile
- Polarization: two parts in conflict, each becoming more extreme
- Unburdening: exile releases carried pain, beliefs, or roles
